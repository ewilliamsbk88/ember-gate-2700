Ember Gate Studios is an independent video game development company founded by three industry veterans who left larger studios to build something of their own. The multiplayer netcode is both the game's biggest selling point and its biggest risk. The Four Functions

During this simulation, you will be assigned to one of four organizational functions. Each function has a specific communication focus and set of audiences.

Leadership

Focus: Strategic communication with investors, partners, and other executives.

Primary audiences: Diana Reyes (investor), StreamerHouse (partner), other founders

Key challenges: Maintaining confidence while being honest about risks. Balancing transparency with strategic positioning.

Development

Focus: Technical communication that non-technical stakeholders can understand.

Primary audiences: Founders, team members, occasionally investors or press

Key challenges: Translating technical complexity into clear assessments. Providing honest timelines without excessive hedging.

Community

Focus: Public-facing communication with players, backers, and press.

Primary audiences: Discord community, Reddit, Twitter/X, gaming press

Key challenges: Maintaining trust during uncertainty. Managing tone across different platforms. Responding to criticism constructively.

Operations

Focus: Internal communication that keeps the team aligned and functional.

Primary audiences: All employees, HR concerns, cross-functional coordination

Key challenges: Maintaining morale during crunch. Communicating difficult decisions fairly. Documentation and process clarity.

Communication Principles

Throughout this simulation, keep these principles in mind:

Know Your Audience

What does this specific person or group already know? What do they care about? What are they afraid of? What do they need from you?

Choose Your Channel

Email, Slack, public post, phone call? The medium shapes the message. Formal vs. informal. Permanent vs. ephemeral. One-way vs. interactive.

Manage Timing

When does this need to be communicated? Who needs to know first? What happens if this leaks before you're ready?

Maintain Credibility

Don't promise what you can't deliver. Don't hide what will eventually come out. Your reputation is the sum of every communication you send.
